Why Standard Background Checks Fail in Africa

International organizations entering Africa routinely rely on digital databases, LinkedIn profiles and formal references — tools designed for Western employment environments. Africa is different. Records are incomplete. Digital footprints are thin. But the truth is not absent. It lives in the community.

Fragmented Record Systems
Many African nations lack centralized, digitized employment or criminal records. Standard database checks return incomplete results, giving a false sense of security to hiring organizations.
High Mobility Across Borders
Candidates frequently move across DRC, Rwanda, Uganda, Kenya and beyond. Tracing their history across multiple African countries requires deep regional networks — not internet searches.
Reputational & Security Risk
Placing the wrong person in a sensitive role — security, finance, or humanitarian operations — can expose your organization to fraud, espionage, reputational damage or physical threat.
Community Knowledge Is the Intelligence
In Africa, a person's true character is known in their community — their school, neighborhood, church, former colleagues. This intelligence is invisible to outsiders. We are insiders.
"Any firm can run a name through a database.
Only AltiMentum Global can walk into the neighborhood, the school, the former employer — and bring back the truth."

The AltiMentum Deep Screening Process

Our methodology is built on one principle: go where the truth lives. We do not rely on what a person chooses to disclose. We go to the sources that have known them over time — before they knew they were being watched.

Investigation Timeline

Phase 1 · Days 1–3
Intake & Scope Definition
Client brief received. Subject's history mapped. Investigation scope, geography and priority areas defined. All information held under strict confidentiality.
Phase 2 · Days 4–10
Document & Record Verification
Official documents verified — identity, education, employment, licenses. Cross-checked directly with issuing institutions in each country of relevance.
Phase 3 · Days 7–18
Community Field Investigation
On-the-ground interviews and discreet inquiries at schools, neighborhoods, former workplaces, religious institutions and community networks. Public testimonies gathered from those who have known the subject over time.
Phase 4 · Days 15–21
Intelligence Synthesis & Reporting
All gathered intelligence cross-analyzed. Inconsistencies identified. Final report prepared with risk rating, findings summary and supporting evidence — delivered securely to the client.
Phase 5 · On Request
Client Debrief & Advisory
Confidential debrief to discuss findings, answer questions and provide hiring or risk mitigation recommendations based on investigation outcomes.

Community Intelligence Sources

We gather testimony from the following source categories — all handled with strict ethical protocols:

Educational institutions — Primary, secondary and tertiary schools, teachers, administrators, peers and institution records.
Neighborhood & residential community — Long-term neighbors, community leaders, local authorities and residential history.
Current & previous employers — Direct supervisors, HR personnel, peers and exit circumstances — verified independently.
Religious & civic institutions — Churches, mosques, community organizations and civic leadership networks.
Professional networks — Industry peers, professional associations and sector-specific reference contacts.
Open source & media — Public records, local media archives, court records and official gazettes where available.
Cross-border contacts — Regional networks across Grand Lakes, East Africa and the broader continent for multi-country subjects.
