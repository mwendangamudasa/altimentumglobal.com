Why Standard Background Checks Fail in Africa

International organizations entering Africa routinely rely on digital databases and formal references — tools designed for Western environments. Africa is different. Records are incomplete. Digital footprints are thin. But the truth is not absent. It lives in the community.

Fragmented Record Systems
Many African nations lack centralized, digitized employment or criminal records. Standard database checks return incomplete results.
High Mobility Across Borders
Candidates frequently move across DRC, Rwanda, Uganda, Kenya and beyond. Tracing their history requires deep regional networks — not internet searches.
Reputational & Security Risk
Placing the wrong person in a sensitive role — security, finance, or humanitarian operations — can expose your organization to fraud, espionage or physical threat.
Community Knowledge Is the Intelligence
In Africa, a person's true character is known in their community — their school, neighborhood, church, former colleagues. This intelligence is invisible to outsiders. We are insiders.
"Any firm can run a name through a database.
Only AltiMentum Global can walk into the neighborhood, the school, the former employer — and bring back the truth."

The AltiMentum Deep Screening Process

Our methodology: go where the truth lives. We do not rely on what a person chooses to disclose. We go to the sources that have known them over time.

Investigation Timeline

Phase 1 · Days 1–3
Intake & Scope Definition
Client brief received. Subject history mapped. All information held under strict confidentiality.
Phase 2 · Days 4–10
Document & Record Verification
Official documents verified — identity, education, employment, licenses — cross-checked directly with issuing institutions.
Phase 3 · Days 7–18
Community Field Investigation
On-the-ground interviews at schools, neighborhoods, former workplaces and religious institutions. Public testimonies gathered from those who have known the subject over time.
Phase 4 · Days 15–21
Intelligence Synthesis & Report
All intelligence cross-analyzed. Final report with risk rating delivered securely to the client.
Phase 5 · On Request
Client Debrief & Advisory
Confidential debrief discussing findings and providing hiring or risk mitigation recommendations.

Community Intelligence Sources

Educational institutions — Schools, teachers, administrators, peers and institution records.
Neighborhood & residential community — Long-term neighbors, community leaders and local authorities.
Current & previous employers — Direct supervisors, HR personnel and exit circumstances — verified independently.
Religious & civic institutions — Churches, mosques, community organizations and civic leadership networks.
Professional networks — Industry peers, professional associations and sector-specific contacts.
Open source & media — Public records, local media archives, court records and official gazettes.
Cross-border contacts — Regional networks across Grand Lakes, East Africa and the broader continent.
